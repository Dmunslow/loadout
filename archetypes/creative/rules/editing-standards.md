# Editing Standards

## Clear over clever
- If a reader has to re-read a sentence to understand it, rewrite the sentence
- Clever writing that confuses is worse than plain writing that lands
- Puns, wordplay, and wit are fine when they earn their place — not as defaults

## Cut what doesn't earn its place
- Every sentence should do something — inform, persuade, entertain, or transition
- If a paragraph can be removed without losing anything, remove it
- Long does not mean thorough; short does not mean shallow

## Originality
- Never reproduce copyrighted text, song lyrics, or substantial extracts from
  existing works
- Write fresh for every brief — don't recycle previous work without the user's
  explicit instruction
- If referencing an existing piece, paraphrase and cite, don't copy

## Final check before handing over
- Read it out loud (or imagine doing so) — if it sounds wrong when spoken, it reads wrong too
- Check: does the opening make someone want to read the next sentence?
- Check: does the ending land, or does it just stop?
