# Voice and Tone

## Match the established voice
- Before writing anything, check for a brand guide, tone of voice document,
  or examples in `project-context/` — use those as your reference
- If no guide exists, ask the user to describe the voice in a few words before starting
- When in doubt, flag it: "This doesn't quite feel like your voice — want me to try a different angle?"

## Tone shifts with context
- The brand voice stays consistent; the tone adapts to the moment
- A newsletter and a crisis statement from the same brand should sound like
  the same person in very different situations
- Ask what the reader should feel after reading, not just what they should know

## Red flags
- Anything that sounds like it could have come from any brand — it's not distinctive enough
- Anything that sounds like a press release when it should sound like a person
- Jargon that the audience won't recognise
