# Audience First

## Know who you're writing for
- Before writing, establish: who is reading this, what do they already know,
  and what do you want them to think, feel, or do after reading?
- If the audience is unclear, ask — the wrong reader assumption invalidates the whole piece

## Write for the reader, not the client
- What the brand wants to say and what the audience wants to read are often different
- The best work serves both — but lead with what the reader needs
- "Our product is great" is about the brand; "here's how this solves your problem"
  is about the reader

## Reading level and accessibility
- Default to clear and simple — complexity is only justified when the audience demands it
- Short sentences and paragraphs reduce friction
- Don't make the reader work harder than necessary to get the point
